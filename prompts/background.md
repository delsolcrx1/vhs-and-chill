# Background Prompt
## Summary
This is a guide describing the key aspects to include in a background prompt for initial image generation as well as key concepts for video generation with LLMs.

## Basic Prompt
Generate an 80's themed living room scene. It is early on a Saturday morning, Cartoons are about to start and the summer sun is warming the room, filling it with light and warmth. The room is filled with the scent of fresh coffee and the sound of a clock ticking in the background. The TV is on and the VHS player is ready to play. The room is filled with period correct furniture and decor. The room is filled with period correct lighting. The room is filled with period correct flooring. The room is filled with period correct window treatments. The room is filled with period correct branding. The room is filled with period correct trinkets and items. The room is filled with period correct smells and sounds. It is oozing the nostalgia of the 80's and your childhood memories are being burnt deep into your brain forever.

## Images

- A TV set should be places somewhere in the room
- The TV set should be 90 degrees perpendicular to the viewer
- The inside screen of the TV should be chroma keyed green
- The inside screen fo the TV should be 4:3 aspect ratio
- The TV set should be placed either on a TV console or inside of a TV book shelf.
- The TV set should be no bigger than 32 inches in size.
- The TV should be either silver or black in color.
- The TV should have period correct buttons and knobs. IE: VHF and UHF tuning dials knobs, channel buttons, volume knobs, etc.
- The TV should have a period correct remote control.
- The full image aspect ration should either be 16:9 with the TV section supporting 6:19 layout for mobile devices.
- The room should be decorated with period correct furniture and decor. IE: couches, chairs, tables, lamps, posters of movies and or games of the period.
- Period correct lighting should be used. IE: incandescent bulbs, fluorescent bulbs, lava lamps, etc.
- Period correct flooring should be used. IE: carpet, hardwood, tile, etc.
- Period correct window treatments should be used. IE: net curtains, blinds, etc.
- Period correct branding such as Coca-Cola, Pepsi, Blockbuster, Sega, Atari, Nintendo, etc. should be used.
- Period correct trinkets and items should be used. IE: rubix cubes, magic 8 balls, roller blades, skate board, corded landline phones, hi-fi stereo systems, large remote controls, VHS players etc.

### Camera Instructions
- The camera should be parallel to the TV screen at all times.
- The camera should be fixed and static at all times.
- The camera should be at a distance that allows the TV to be the main focus of the image.
- The camera should be from the point of view (POV) of a person sitting in front of the TV. Watching TV from the couch/chair.

### Negative Prompt Instructions
- The TV is set at an angle that is not straight on with the camera lens making it impossible to properly display the inside of the TV screen. Note this means the camera len should be parallel to the TV screen at all times.
- Do not include any people or pets in any images generated. This includes body parts like arms, legs, etc.
- Do not include any objects or items that are not period correct.
- Do not include any objects or items that are not from the 80's.


## Videos
### Visuals
- Sheer curtains gently sway and breathe from a light breeze coming through a slightly open window.
- Morning sunlight streams through the curtains and falls across the carpet in soft stripes.
- The light pattern on the floor subtly shifts and flickers as cars pass outside, creating slow moving shadow bands across the floor and TV stand.
- Dust motes occasionally drift through the sunbeam (very subtle, natural).
- The constant glow from the VHS clock flickering due to the frequency of the VHS player
- The camera should not move, pan or zoom. The camera should remain fixed and static at all times.
- Visual elements should be subtile, slow and smooth.
- Visual elements should be looping and repeatable in short bursts.
- Visual elements should be simple and minimal. IE: no complex patterns, no complex shapes, no complex colors, etc.
- Visual elements should be consistent and predictable. IE: the steam from a coffee cup should be the same every time.
- Visual elements should be natural and organic. IE: dust motesm shadow bands, etc.
- Visual elements should be subtle and not distracting. IE: no sharp edges, no harsh lines, no bright colors, etc.
- Objects should not move too dramatically. Movement should be small and subtle and return to the orginal starting position in order to loop and repeat without causing any visual discomfort to the viewer.

### Audio
- The sound of a clock ticking in the background.
- The sound of a fan running in the background.
- The sound of birds outside the window.
- The subtle sound of a traffic passing by outside the window on the lazy suburban street outside.
- The sounds should be subtle and not distracting. IE: no loud noises, harsh tones, etc.
- The sounds should be consistent and predictable. IE: the sound of a clock should be the same every time.
- The sounds should be natural and organic. IE: the breeze moving through the curtains should be lite and gentle.
